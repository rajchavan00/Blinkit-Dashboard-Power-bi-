Blinkit Sales Analytics

## 1. Project Title / Headline
🛒 Blinkit Sales Analytics: India's Last Minute App Dashboard A dynamic, interactive data visualization tool built to explore grocery delivery sales performance—focusing on item categories, outlet characteristics, and consumer purchasing preferences.

## 2. Short Description / Purpose
The Blinkit Sales Analytics Dashboard is a visually engaging and analytical Power BI report designed to help users explore and compare sales metrics across different outlet sizes, location tiers, and product categories. The dashboard focuses on highlighting major retail metrics like total sales ($1.20M), average rating (3.92), fat content distribution, and historical outlet establishment trends. This tool is intended for use by retail analysts, supply chain managers, inventory planners, and data-driven strategists who seek to understand consumer behavior and outlet performance in the quick-commerce sector.

## 3. Tech Stack
The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:

📊 Power BI Desktop – Main data visualization platform used for report creation.

📂 Power Query – Data transformation and cleaning layer for reshaping and preparing the raw grocery data.

🧠 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Used for calculated measures, dynamic visuals, and conditional logic to calculate total sales, averages, and item counts.

📝 Data Modeling – Relationships established among sales, item, and outlet tables to enable seamless cross-filtering and aggregation.

📁 File Format – .pbix for development and .png for dashboard previews.

## 4. Data Source
Source: Kaggle (Blinkit Grocery Data).

Data contains detailed records of over 9,000 retail items, including details on their fat content, item type, visibility, and sales figures. It also includes comprehensive outlet attributes such as size (Small, Medium, High), location tier (Tier 1, 2, 3), and establishment year (spanning from 2012 to 2022).

## 5. Features / Highlights
Business Problem The quick-commerce and grocery delivery industry operates on thin margins and high volumes, yet operators often lack an intuitive way to identify which product categories and store setups yield the highest returns. Key questions such as: Which outlet types generate the most sales? Are consumers preferring low-fat or regular products? How does outlet location (Tier 1 vs Tier 3) affect revenue? ... are difficult to answer quickly with raw spreadsheet data.

Goal of the Dashboard To deliver an interactive visual tool that:

Enables users to explore grocery sales globally across the Blinkit network.

Supports decisions such as inventory stocking, outlet expansion, and marketing focus.

Uncovers trends in consumer health preferences and store format efficiency.

Walkthrough of Key Visuals

Key KPIs (Top Left): Quick-glance cards showing Total Sales ($1.20M), Average Sales ($140), Total Number of Items (9K), and Average Customer Rating (3.92).

Filter Panel (Left): Interactive slicers let users filter all visuals globally by Outlet Size, Outlet Location (Tier), and specific Item Types.

Outlet Establishment (Line Chart): Tracks the growth of outlet establishments over a decade, highlighting a massive spike in growth around 2018.

Fat Content by Outlet & Overall (Donut/Bar Charts): Compares "Low Fat" vs. "Regular" item sales, helping identify health-conscious purchasing trends across Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 locations.

Item Type Distribution (Bar Chart): Ranks product categories, revealing that Fruits and Vegetables (1.23K) and Snack Foods (1.20K) are the top-selling items.

Outlet Size & Location (Donut & Funnel Chart): Visualizes the distribution of Medium, Small, and High outlet sizes, alongside Tier 1, 2, and 3 geographical performance.

Outlet Type Matrix (Table): Compares store formats (Supermarket Type 1, 2, 3, and Grocery Store) against Total Sales, No. of Items, and Rating. It clearly highlights Supermarket Type 1 as the dominant revenue driver ($787.55K).

Business Impact & Insights

Inventory Optimization: Supply chain managers can ensure high-demand categories like Fruits/Vegetables and Snack Foods are prioritized in warehousing and delivery routes.

Strategic Expansion: By seeing the dominance of Tier 3 locations and Supermarket Type 1 formats, business developers can strategically plan future outlet structures.

Consumer Preference Tracking: The breakdown of Low Fat vs. Regular items allows for targeted marketing campaigns and product procurement based on regional dietary trends.
